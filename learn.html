How do AI tools
actually work? 🤖

People say AI is super complicated — but the heart of it is just maths I already know: multiplying, adding, percentages, averages, and the guess-and-check trick from school! Let me show you how a tool like the one I code with (grown-ups call it a "large language model") is really just a giant next-word guesser. Every number below is one you could check yourself. ✏️

1
🔮 the big idea

It's just guessing the next word

When you type "The cat sat on the ___", the AI doesn't really know the answer. It guesses which word comes next. Then it adds that word, and guesses the next one, and the next… until it has written a whole sentence. That's the big secret — one little guess at a time.

2
✖️➕ multiply & add

It gives each word points

To pick the next word, it writes down a few choices and gives each one points. Each choice has some clues, and each clue has an importance number (grown-ups call it a "weight"). You multiply each clue by its importance and add them up. That's the whole sum!

Let's score three choices for "The cat sat on the ___" — mat, dog, sky. We have two clues, and each clue's importance is 5:

🔎 Clue A — does it fit the word "on"?  ·  🐱 Clue B — does it match a sentence about a cat?  (each scored out of 10)

mat: 5×8 + 5×4 = 60 dog: 5×4 + 5×2 = 30 sky: 5×1 + 5×1 = 10

So mat gets the most points. Just multiplying and adding! 🎉

3
💯 percentages

Turning points into "out of 100"

Points are easier to compare as percentages — "out of 100". Add all the points together: 60 + 30 + 10 = 100. So mat = 60/100 = 60%, dog = 30%, sky = 10%. The highest one usually wins, so the AI picks mat. 🐈

4
🔁 guess & check

How did it get good? Guess and check — millions of times

Here's the clever part: those importance numbers didn't start out smart. They began as random guesses! The AI reads a real sentence where it already knows the true next word, sees how wrong its guess was, and nudges the numbers to be a little less wrong. Then it does it again… and again. It's exactly the guess-and-check method from maths class — just done millions of times.

5
➗ average

Why it reads SO much

To get those numbers right, it can't just learn from one sentence — one sentence might be weird or unusual. So it reads a huge number of them and basically takes an average, so the usual next word wins instead of a strange one-off.

For example: if "mat" came next in 8 out of 10 cat-sentences, that's a strong average — so "mat" is the safe guess. More examples → a steadier average → better guesses. 📚

6
🚀 scale

It's the same maths — just BIGGER

A real AI tool does exactly this: list the choices, multiply each clue by its importance, add them up, turn it into percentages, and learn the importance numbers by guess-and-check. The only difference is how many numbers — not 2 clues, but millions of them.

💡 So what does that mean?

The maths I learn at school — multiplying, adding, percentages, averages, and guess-and-check — really is the engine inside AI. It's not magic. It's just a LOT of small sums, done very, very fast. 💙
