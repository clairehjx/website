About me

A little about me 💙

Hi! My name is Claire and I'm a Primary 5 student. I love problem solving — that's a fancy way of saying I enjoy breaking big, tricky problems into small steps a computer can follow.

When I code, I like to look for patterns, figure out the algorithm (the recipe of steps), and then watch my idea come to life on the screen. My favourite colour is blue and I love watercolour art, so I made this whole website feel like a soft blue painting.

I build my projects with Claude Code as my AI coding partner, and I'm also testing out Gemini Antigravity 2.0. I think it's really cool to compare different AI tools and learn what each one is best at — that's part of how I figure things out.

This is my little corner of the internet where I keep the apps I build and share my coding adventure. Thanks for visiting! 🎨

My coding journey

How my adventure is going 🧭

THE START

I got curious about how apps work

I started wondering: how do the games and apps on my screen actually know what to do? So I began learning to write code and give the computer instructions, one step at a time.

MY FIRST APP

Pokémon Coloring Book 🎨

I built a coloring app where you click a Pokémon to paint it. The clever part is the flood-fill algorithm — when you click, my code spreads out to every connected pixel of the same colour, just like the paint-bucket tool. I also added an undo button and a watercolour brush effect!

🧩 The tricky part: when I clicked to colour, the paint kept leaking past the outlines, because the picture had lots of slightly different shades of the same colour. How I solved it: I let the fill accept colours that are close enough instead of exactly the same, and I treated the dark outlines as walls so the paint stops at the lines.

RIGHT NOW

Learning more & tidying my code

I'm learning to make my websites look modern, keep my projects organised in separate places on GitHub, and explain my ideas clearly — which is a skill all on its own!

🧩 The tricky part: I wanted every page — my homepage and all my apps — to share the exact same watercolour-blue look, but copying the colours into each file got messy and easy to break. How I solved it: I saved all my colours once as reusable variables, so changing one line updates the whole site at once.

NOW LIVE

A science tutoring tool 🔬

I built a tool that turns real exam questions into friendly PSLE science cheat sheets — connecting what I know about coding with what I'm learning at school.

🧩 The tricky part: the tool sometimes picked questions that weren't really about the topic I asked for, so the cheat sheets had the wrong things on them. How I solved it: I added a checking step that double-checks every question matches the topic before it's allowed onto the cheat sheet.
