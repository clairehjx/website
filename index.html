About me

A bit about me

My name is Claire — not a maths genius, despite popular belief (looking at you, MATURE YOUNG LADY WHO WILL GO VERY FAR, and a certain MOUSY) — and I'm a Primary 5 student. My hobby is problem solving: basically I take a big(ish) problem and break it into small steps a computer can follow. Easier said than done.

My favourite colour is blue and I'll take watercolour over any other style, so I made this whole site feel like a soft blue painting. (I tried, anyway.)

The way I see it, AI is just reading and maths smushed together — so honestly I might as well be AI, because that's roughly how I spend half my LIFE.

(For the record: I wrote this part myself. The AI version came out way too cheerful and SO not me.)

Shophouse Row

My biggest one yet: a cozy pixel-art game for my friends. Everyone runs their own shophouse on a shared street — you walk around in a little top-down world, decorate your shop (and a secret 2nd floor), draw your own items in a pixel editor, then visit friends to leave notes, trade, and give gifts. It's real multiplayer, so we can all play together on our own phones.

Science Tutoring Tool

A tool that turns real exam papers into PSLE cheat sheets — a team-up with my dad, and honestly I was a reluctant collaborator (I'd much rather have built something more fun). Still, it works: I focused on the open-ended questions, because the hardest part of science is getting the exact keywords into your answer, so each sheet flags the keywords that matter (and the easiest places to lose marks).
