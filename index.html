My coding journey

How it's going so far

THE START

It started with a question

I kept wondering how the games and apps on my screen actually KNOW what to do. Turns out you have to spell out every single step for the computer, one painfully small instruction at a time — so I started learning to do exactly that.

MY FIRST APP

Pokémon Coloring Book

My first proper app: click a Pokémon and it paints it in. The clever bit is the flood-fill algorithm — one click and my code spreads out to every connected pixel of the same colour, like the paint-bucket tool. There's an undo button and a watercolour brush too. (It took more tries than I'd like to admit.)

RIGHT NOW

Tidying up and learning more

These days I'm making my sites look properly modern, keeping every project in its own place on GitHub, and learning to explain my ideas without rambling — which is somehow harder than the actual coding. My AI “pet” cheers me on the entire time (it never, ever stops).

NOW LIVE

A science tutoring tool

I built a tool that turns real exam papers into PSLE science cheat sheets — basically connecting my coding with the stuff I'm learning at school. I focused on the open-ended questions, because getting the exact keywords into your answer is the hardest part (and the easiest place to lose marks).
